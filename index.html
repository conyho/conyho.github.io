About

Hello — I'm Cony.

I am an Assistant Professor of Marketing in the College of Business at Florida Atlantic University. My research sits at the intersection of consumer psychology and marketing strategy, asking questions like: What makes people donate more generously? Why do consumers embrace some brands and reject others? How do digital formats, AI, and virtual influencers change the way we buy?

My work has been published in journals including the Journal of Applied Psychology, European Journal of Marketing, Journal of Business Research, and Psychology & Marketing. Before joining FAU, I held faculty positions at Northern Arizona University, Clemson University, and the University of Hartford.

Outside the office, I have a strong passion for sports, bubble tea, and League of Legends. Fun fact: before coming to the United States, I was the youngest FIFA World Cup commentator on ELTA TV in Taiwan during the 2010 World Cup.

Research Interests

What I study

🤝

Prosocial Behavior

How framing, meaning, and candidate positioning shape charitable giving — from time-framed donation appeals to why consumers avoid socially responsible gifts.

🏷️

Branding Strategy

How consumers relate to brands: servant brands, brand revitalization, and why "flawless" virtual influencers can backfire on brand attitudes.

📱

Digital Marketing

Consumer responses to digital collections, AI-produced art, chatbots, and the psychology of spending in digital environments.
